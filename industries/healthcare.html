Healthcare-Specific IT

IT That Keeps Your Practice Running — and Compliant

Medical practices face a unique IT challenge: your systems must be fast and reliable enough for clinical workflows, yet locked down tightly enough to protect patient health information (PHI) under HIPAA.

A system that's down during a patient visit isn't just inconvenient — it's a risk to patient safety and your revenue. A misconfigured system that exposes PHI isn't just a headache — it's a federal violation with fines starting at $100 per record.

Corstar Communications has been supporting Westchester healthcare organizations since the early days of electronic health records. We understand the clinical environment, the compliance landscape, and the systems your staff uses every day.

HIPAA Compliance

We Handle the Technical Side of HIPAA

The HIPAA Security Rule requires covered entities to implement technical, physical, and administrative safeguards for PHI. We handle the technical safeguards completely.

🔐

Access Controls

Role-based access, unique user IDs, automatic logoffs, and encryption for PHI in transit and at rest.

📋

Audit Controls

Hardware and software activity logs that record and track access to PHI, as required by HIPAA.

🛡️

Integrity Controls

Mechanisms to ensure PHI is not improperly altered or destroyed — including backup and version control.

🌐

Transmission Security

Encrypted email, secure file transfer, and VPN for all PHI transmitted over any network.

🚨

Incident Response

A documented process for detecting, responding to, and reporting security incidents involving PHI.

📄

Documentation

Audit-ready documentation of all technical safeguards — exactly what OCR looks for in a HIPAA investigation.

Why It Matters Now

Healthcare Is the #1 Target for Ransomware

The healthcare sector experiences more ransomware attacks than any other industry. Clinical data is worth 10–40x more on the black market than financial data. A single breach of a small practice can result in HHS fines, legal liability, and reputational damage that takes years to recover from.

We help Westchester medical practices build defenses that work — without disrupting clinical operations or requiring your staff to become IT experts.
