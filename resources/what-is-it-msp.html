What Is an IT MSP?

An IT MSP (Managed Service Provider) is a company that manages your business technology on an ongoing basis for a flat monthly fee. Instead of calling someone when something breaks, an MSP monitors your systems proactively, resolves issues before they cause downtime, and handles all aspects of your IT infrastructure — from cybersecurity to cloud services to help desk support.

The term "MSP" stands for Managed Service Provider. In the context of IT, it describes a third-party company that takes responsibility for your technology systems — acting as your outsourced IT department, or supplementing an internal IT team.

MSPs became common in the early 2000s as businesses realized that reactive "break-fix" IT was unpredictable and expensive. Today, managed IT services are the standard model for small and mid-size businesses that want reliable technology without the cost of hiring a full in-house IT team.

What Does an IT MSP Do?

A good IT MSP does far more than fix computers. At its core, an MSP assumes ongoing responsibility for the health, security, and performance of your technology environment. This includes:

Proactive Monitoring

MSPs use Remote Monitoring and Management (RMM) software to watch your servers, workstations, and network devices around the clock. When a drive is failing, a server is running hot, or a patch hasn't been applied, your MSP knows about it — and fixes it — before your staff does.

Cybersecurity Management

A modern IT MSP provides layered cybersecurity: managed firewalls, endpoint detection and response (EDR), email security, multi-factor authentication, dark web monitoring, DNS filtering, and employee security training. Cybersecurity isn't a separate service — it's built into managed IT.

Help Desk Support

Your employees get a phone number and ticketing system to reach real engineers when something isn't working. MSP help desks resolve most issues remotely within hours, and dispatch on-site technicians when needed.

Cloud Services Management

MSPs manage Microsoft 365, Azure, cloud backup, and other cloud platforms. This includes provisioning users, managing licenses, configuring security policies, and administering SharePoint, Teams, and OneDrive.

Strategic IT Planning

A quality MSP acts as a virtual CIO (vCIO) — helping you plan hardware refresh cycles, budget for technology, evaluate new software, and align your IT roadmap with your business goals.

MSP vs. Break-Fix IT: What's the Difference?

The two primary models for business IT support are managed services and break-fix. Here's how they compare:

Feature IT MSP (Managed Services) Break-Fix IT
Pricing model Flat monthly fee per user Hourly rate when called
Monitoring ✓ 24/7 proactive ✗ None
Cybersecurity ✓ Included ✗ Separate, extra cost
Cost predictability ✓ Predictable monthly cost ✗ Variable, spikes on failures
Downtime approach ✓ Prevent before it happens ✗ React after it happens
Strategic planning ✓ vCIO guidance included ✗ Not offered
Best for Businesses that depend on technology Businesses with minimal IT needs

For most Westchester businesses with 10 or more employees, managed IT services cost less over time than break-fix — because preventing a server failure is far cheaper than recovering from one.

What's Included in Managed IT Services?

Managed IT packages vary by provider, but a comprehensive MSP plan should include:

  • 24/7 remote monitoring of servers, workstations, and network devices
  • Automated patch management — operating system and third-party software updates
  • Managed endpoint detection and response (EDR/antivirus replacement)
  • Managed firewall and DNS filtering
  • Email security — anti-phishing, anti-spam, and advanced threat protection
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) deployment and management
  • Dark web monitoring for compromised credentials
  • Help desk support — remote and on-site
  • Microsoft 365 administration
  • Cloud backup and verified recovery testing
  • Employee security awareness training
  • Monthly reporting and strategic IT reviews

How Much Does an IT MSP Cost?

Most MSPs price managed IT services per user, per month. For small and mid-size businesses in Westchester County, comprehensive managed IT with cybersecurity typically runs $80–$150 per user per month.

The exact price depends on:

  • Number of users and devices
  • Your compliance requirements (HIPAA, NY DFS, etc.)
  • Which services are included (cloud management, advanced security, etc.)
  • Whether you need on-site support or remote-only

A business with 20 users paying $100/user/month spends $2,000/month on managed IT. That same business hiring a single in-house IT person costs $70,000–$90,000/year in salary alone — without tools, training, benefits, or coverage gaps when that person is out.

The MSP model delivers more expertise, more coverage, and more security for less than the cost of one in-house hire.

Does My Business Need an IT MSP?

An IT MSP is a strong fit if your business:

  • Has 5–200 employees and depends on computers, email, or cloud software to operate
  • Handles sensitive data — patient records, client information, financial data
  • Has compliance requirements (HIPAA, NY DFS Cybersecurity Regulation, SOC 2)
  • Has experienced IT issues that caused downtime or disruption
  • Is growing and needs scalable IT infrastructure
  • Currently uses a break-fix provider with unpredictable costs
  • Has no dedicated IT staff, or a small team that is overwhelmed

Industries in Westchester that most commonly work with IT MSPs: healthcare, legal, financial services, manufacturing, nonprofit, and professional services.

How to Choose an IT MSP in Westchester

Not all MSPs are the same. Here are the most important factors to evaluate:

Local presence and response time

A Westchester-based MSP can dispatch technicians on-site quickly when remote support isn't enough. Ask what their guaranteed response time is for critical issues — it should be 1–4 hours for outages.

Security-first approach

Cybersecurity should be built into your managed IT plan, not sold as an optional extra. Ask if endpoint detection and response, email security, and MFA are included — not billed separately.

Industry experience

If you're in healthcare, legal, or financial services, your MSP needs to understand your compliance requirements. Ask for specific examples of clients in your industry and what compliance frameworks they support.

References and longevity

An MSP that has been in business for decades has proven it can sustain client relationships and adapt through technology changes. Ask for client references you can actually call.

Transparent contracts

Understand what's included, what's excluded, and what happens at contract renewal. Avoid MSPs with opaque pricing or auto-renewal clauses with large price increases.

Frequently Asked Questions About IT MSPs

MSP stands for Managed Service Provider. In IT, an MSP is a company that manages your technology infrastructure and services on an ongoing basis, typically for a flat monthly subscription fee. The MSP model replaces or supplements in-house IT staff with a team of outside engineers who monitor, maintain, and secure your systems proactively.
An "IT company" is a broad term — it can mean any technology business, including software developers, hardware vendors, or IT consultants. An IT MSP specifically refers to a company that provides ongoing, proactive management of your technology under a managed services agreement. The key distinctions are: ongoing monitoring (not just one-time projects), a flat monthly fee (not hourly billing), and a focus on preventing problems rather than fixing them after the fact.
Onboarding with an IT MSP typically takes 2–4 weeks. During that time, the MSP documents your environment, deploys monitoring and security tools, migrates your support requests into their help desk, and reviews your systems for issues. Well-run onboardings cause minimal disruption to your business. Corstar's onboarding process gets most clients fully operational within 30 days.
Most small and mid-size businesses do not need an in-house IT person when they have a full-service MSP. The MSP handles everything from help desk to strategic planning. Some larger organizations use an MSP alongside an internal IT director who focuses on business alignment and vendor management, while the MSP handles day-to-day operations. This hybrid model can be very effective.
Microsoft 365 is a software platform — a set of cloud tools (email, Teams, Office apps, SharePoint). An MSP is a service provider that manages and secures your use of those tools. An MSP configures your Microsoft 365 tenant, enforces security policies, manages user accounts, supports your staff, and ensures your Microsoft 365 environment is compliant and optimized. The two are complementary: cloud platforms provide the tools, and your MSP makes them work securely and reliably.
